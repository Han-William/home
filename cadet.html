Air Cadets

What is Air Cadets? Royal Canadian Air Cadets is an extracurricular program that I am apart of. The aim of the program is to foster an interst in aviation, to get youths fit at a young age and to build leadership skills. There are many clubs within the Cadets’s program such as the drill team, biathlon and effective speeking to name a few. The end goal of the program for any young air cadet is to learn how to fly a plane. There are many lessons in the Air Cadet program that teaches everything one needs to know about a plane and they are truly fascinating. Meetings are usually on Tuesdays at six fourty–five.

What are my experiences in the Air Cadets program?I am part of the 110 Black Hawk Squadron and my experiences in the program were net positive by a long shot. I was able to learn about things that just dosen’t cross your mind often. I was also able to attend the field training exercise in November, 2019. It was the second time in my life that I was able to see the stars in the sky. Before the Covid crisis I took part in the effective speaking competition and it was quite memorable. Even during the Covid-crisis I took part in two virtual summer courses one on drone piloting and one on Effective Speaking. This year I have been taking part in virtual meets hosted by the squadron and am still learning skills. Those skills incude polishing boots and ironing uniforms amongst other things. I own many things to the Royal Canadian Air Cadets and I always by the motto of " Spirit, Effort, Success".
